Olympic bike - the bike with five wheels

Why was it made?

Firstly for my pleasure, on the other hand on the occassion of the Olympic Games of 2004.

The OLYMPIC BIKE is a vehicle, but it can be declared as a kinetic sculpture, because the placing and colour of the 5 wheels suggests the five rings of the Olimpic simbols.

How does it work?

Every wheel is rotating when we are riding the bike.

How was it made?

The base of the OLYMPIC BIKE is a Camping bike, with wheels of 20 inches.
I rebuilt the fork into a double fork constructed on above the other. The wheel mounted on the upper fork is moved by friction with the wheel in the lower one.

In the rear i mounted a fork from a 28 inch bike so the upper wheel in the back is also moved by friction.

The middle part of the framework was completely rebuilt. The chainwheel of the middle wheel is connected by a chain to the chainwheel which receives drive from the pedals.

How can I brake with this vehicle?

The OLYMPIC BIKE has three independently working brakes:

  1. a coaster brake, controlling the back wheel by foot.
  2. the driving brake controlling by right hand braking the lower back wheel.
  3. the driving brake controlled by the left hand which stops the middle wheel.
